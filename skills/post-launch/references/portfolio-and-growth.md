# Portfolio and Business Growth

Every delivered project becomes a portfolio piece and a growth opportunity. This file covers case study writing, image capture, portfolio hosting, the first 3 projects strategy, referral system, Google reviews, business tools, and pricing evolution.

Write case studies within 1 week of launch while the details are fresh.

---

## What a Good Case Study Includes

1. **Client and problem** — who they are, what they needed, what wasn't working (2–3 sentences)
2. **Your process** — discovery → research → design → build (3–4 sentences, no jargon)
3. **Key design decisions** — 1–2 specific choices you made and why they served the user or business goal (shows thinking, not just execution)
4. **The result** — what launched, what the client said, any measurable outcome
5. **Before/after** — screenshot comparison if it's a redesign
6. **Live site link** — always link to the real thing

**Target length:** 300–500 words. Let the screenshots do the heavy lifting.

```markdown
[1–2 sentences describing the core problem — what was missing,

## What I Did

I started by [first step — discovery / audit / research]. From

[specific design or technical problem] — I solved it by [solution].

## Key Design Decisions

**[Decision 1]:** [What I chose and why — tied to user or business goal]

**[Decision 2]:** [What I chose and why]

## The Result

[What launched — pages, features, key outcomes]. The client said:
"[direct quote if you have one]." [Any measurable result — ranked
for X keyword, client reported more calls, etc.]

[Live site: [URL]]
```

## Getting the Images You Need

**Browser screenshots:**
- **Screely** (screely.com) — wraps screenshots in a browser frame, looks professional
- **Shots.so** — similar, more style options
- **Chrome DevTools** — full-page screenshots at exact viewport widths (360px, 768px, 1280px)

**Device mockups:**
- **Mockuphone** (mockuphone.com) — place screenshots inside phone/laptop frames, free
- **Smartmockups** — more options, free tier available

**Screen recordings:**
- **Loom** (free) — 60-second walkthrough of the live site; shows it working, not just static; free tier is sufficient

**Real photos (most powerful):**
- Visit the client's business and photograph the site on their actual device in their real context
- A photo of a plumber looking at his new website on his phone in front of his truck is worth 10 browser screenshots

**Before/after:**
- Screenshot the old site before you touch anything
- Use the same viewport and same page for a clean comparison

## The First 3 Projects Strategy

Your first 3 projects set the trajectory of everything that follows. Be deliberate.

**Project 1 — Build your proof**
Offer to a friend, family member, or your own freelance business at a steep discount or free. In exchange: full creative control, a detailed case study, a Google review, and permission to use it in your portfolio. Your hackathon project, your existing freelance project, or any prior work counts here.

**Project 2 — Prove you can work with a real client**
A real paying client at your starter rate ($500–$900 for a simple site). Proves you can deliver for a stranger, manage a real relationship, and handle feedback professionally.

**Project 3 — Aim higher**
A slightly more complex project or a more visible business — something that becomes your strongest case study and sets the tone for the next tier of clients.

After 3 solid case studies your conversion rate on discovery calls improves significantly because you can show proof instead of describing potential.

## Referral System

Word of mouth is the #1 source of new clients for local web designers. Build a system around it.

### When to Ask

The best moment is right after a client expresses satisfaction — in the call, in a message, or when they see the site live for the first time.

**In the moment (verbal):**
> "That's great to hear. If you know any other local business owners who might need a website, I'd really appreciate the introduction. A lot of my best work comes from referrals like that."

**Post-delivery (combined with Google review):**
The SKILL.md post-delivery sequence combines the Google review and referral ask into one message sent 3–5 days after delivery. Use that as the primary script.

**Follow-up check-in (1–2 weeks after launch):**
```
Subject: Quick check-in — [Business Name] site

Hi [Name],

It's been [a week] since we launched — how's everything looking?
Any small tweaks you want to make?

Also, if you know any other business owners who could use a website,
I'd love an introduction. A simple "you should talk to Dereck"
goes a long way, and I'll take great care of anyone you send my way.

Thanks again — it was a great project.

Dereck
(408) 759-0778
```

### Referral Incentive (Optional)

If you want to formalize it:
- **Cash:** $50–$100 for a referral that becomes a paying client
- **Service credit:** One month free maintenance
- Mention it once, don't pressure. Keep it simple.

## Business Tools and Infrastructure

Set this up before you have 2 active projects running simultaneously.

### Invoicing and Contracts
- **Wave** (free) — invoicing, recurring billing, basic income tracking. Start here.
- **Bonsai** (~$17/month) — contracts + proposals + invoices in one. Upgrade when volume warrants.
- **HelloSign** (free tier) — e-signatures if you write your own agreements

### Project Management
- **Notion** (free) — one page per client: status, links, notes, checklist. Works well for 1–5 active clients.
- **Trello** (free) — Kanban board for project stages (Discovery → Design → Build → Review → Delivered)

### File Management
- **Google Drive** (free) — shared folders per client for asset exchange

**Folder structure per project:**
```
[Client Name] — [Project]/
├── 01 - Discovery/     ← questionnaire, notes, research report
├── 02 - Design/        ← wireframes, specs, style guide
├── 03 - Assets/        ← logos, photos, fonts from client
├── 04 - Development/   ← code files
├── 05 - Delivery/      ← final files, README, handoff docs
└── 06 - Admin/         ← contract, invoices, proposal
```

### Time Tracking
- **Toggl Track** (free) — track time per project and client
- Even on flat-rate projects, tracking time reveals whether you're profitable
- If a $600 project took 40 hours, that's $15/hour — price that project type higher next time

### Communication
- Pick one channel per client and stick to it — email is safest (paper trail)
- Text/WhatsApp for quick questions only — never manage feedback in text
- Set a response standard upfront: reply within 24 hours on weekdays

### Professional Email
- Gmail works for now
- When dereckvilla.design is live, upgrade to Google Workspace ($6/month) or Zoho Mail (free)
- `dereck@dereckvilla.design` is a meaningful credibility upgrade, especially for Bay Area clients

## Pricing Evolution

Your current starting prices are intentionally reasonable to build your portfolio quickly. Raise them systematically.

| Stage | When | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Starting out | Now | Use current pricing table from `client-onboarding` |
| Early traction | After 3–5 completed projects with case studies | Raise all floors by 30–50% |
| Established | After 10+ projects | Revisit based on market feedback and demand |
| Specialized | When you have a niche (trades, restaurants, Bay Area tech) | Premium pricing for your niche |

**Raise prices confidently.** When you get a client without them hesitating at your price, you're undercharging. The goal is to occasionally hear "that's a bit more than I expected" — that's the signal you're priced correctly.

**Bay Area clients:** Already add 20–35% to Central Valley rates. As your Bay Area case studies grow, lean into this market — budgets are higher and a portfolio of Bay Area work commands premium rates.
