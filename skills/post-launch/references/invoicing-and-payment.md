Contracts + proposals + invoices + time tracking in one place. Worth it when you have 3+ active clients or want everything integrated.

**For payment collection:**

- **Cash / check** — always issue a written invoice. Cash income is taxable income regardless of whether a 1099 is issued.

## When to Send Each Invoice

| Invoice | When | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| Deposit invoice | Immediately after agreement is signed | 50% of total |
| Final invoice | When project is complete — before files transfer or site goes live | 50% of total |
| Maintenance invoice | 1st of each month | Monthly plan rate |
| Change order invoice | After change order work is completed | Change order amount |
| Late fee | 14 days after original due date | $25 or 5% — whichever is greater |

**Never start work without a cleared deposit. Never deliver files without a cleared final payment.**

## Late Payment Handling

The scripts in `post-launch/SKILL.md` cover the day 7, 14, and 21 sequence. This section covers what to do beyond that, and how to handle disputes, chargebacks, and collections.

### Evidence to Keep From Day One

Before a payment is ever late, make sure you have these on file for every project:

- Signed agreement (email confirmation counts — screenshot it)
- The original proposal with scope and price
- All invoice copies with sent timestamps
- Delivery confirmation — a message, email, or screenshot showing the client received the work
- Any scope change agreements in writing
- All client communications (keep email threads; screenshot any texts)

If a dispute happens, this paper trail is your entire case. Without it, you have nothing.

### After Day 21 — Escalation Path

**Step 1 — Final written notice (Day 28)**
Send a formal email, not a text. State the amount owed, the original due date, the contract date, and that you're preparing to file in small claims court. Keep it factual, not emotional.

```
Subject: Formal Notice — Outstanding Invoice #[X]

[Name],

This is formal notice that $[amount] remains unpaid under our
agreement dated [date] for web design services. The original
due date was [date]. This invoice is now [X] days past due.

If payment is not received by [date 7 days from now], I will
file a claim in San Joaquin County Small Claims Court without
further notice.

Dereck Villagrana
(408) 759-0778
```

**Step 2 — Small claims court (if no response)**
- California small claims handles up to $12,500
- San Joaquin County filing fee: $30–$75 depending on claim amount
- File at the courthouse in Stockton: 180 E Weber Ave
- Bring: printed copies of the agreement, all invoices, delivery proof, and all written communications
- The judge will ask both sides to speak — keep it factual: "We agreed on X, I delivered Y, they haven't paid Z"
- The threat of filing alone resolves about 80% of cases at this stage

**Step 3 — Collections agency (for amounts over $500)**
If small claims feels like too much effort for a smaller amount, a collections agency will pursue the debt for a percentage (typically 25–40% of what's recovered). Not ideal, but better than nothing for a debt you've already written off. Use only as a last resort — it permanently ends the client relationship.

### Chargeback Handling (PayPal / Credit Card)

If a client disputes a charge through PayPal, their credit card company, or a payment processor:

**Act within 24–48 hours.** Every platform has a response window — missing it means you automatically lose the dispute.

**What to submit as evidence:**
- The signed agreement or email confirmation of scope and price
- Invoice copy
- Delivery confirmation (screenshot of message saying work was received, or the live URL)
- Any communications showing the client was satisfied with the work
- The IP address or login record showing they accessed delivered files (if you have it)

**PayPal specifically:** Disputes go through their Resolution Center. Log in, find the dispute, and upload evidence directly. PayPal typically sides with the seller when there's a clear digital delivery with a paper trail.

**The best defense against chargebacks:** Never accept credit card payments for large amounts without a signed agreement, and always get written confirmation of delivery before closing a project.

### When to Write It Off

Sometimes the math doesn't work. If the outstanding amount is under $200 and the client is unresponsive, the time and stress of pursuing it may cost more than the debt. Write it off, learn from it, and tighten your process:

- Did you start work without a cleared deposit? Fix that.
- Did you deliver before confirming final payment? Fix that.
- Did you skip a written agreement? Fix that.

Every bad payment experience should harden one part of your process.

## Tracking Your Income

Keep a simple log — even a spreadsheet works:

| Invoice # | Client | Amount | Sent | Paid | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 001 | Rodriguez Plumbing | $450 | 3/1 | 3/3 | Deposit |
| 002 | Rodriguez Plumbing | $450 | 3/28 | 3/29 | Final |
| 003 | Manteca Tile Co | $350 | 4/5 | — | Deposit — pending |

**Why this matters:** All freelance income is taxable — even Zelle and cash payments. Keeping a clear log means no surprises and makes filing straightforward.

**Set aside 25–30% of every payment for taxes.** Open a separate savings account and transfer that percentage immediately each time you get paid. Don't touch it until estimated tax payments are due (quarterly: April, June, September, January).

## Deductible Business Expenses to Track

Keep receipts or records for all of these — they reduce your taxable income:

- Software subscriptions (Adobe, Figma, Wave, Bonsai, Notion)
- Domain purchases and hosting fees
- Stock photos, fonts, icon packs
- Home office (if you use a dedicated space — percentage of rent/utilities)
- Internet bill (business use percentage)
- Phone bill (business use percentage)
- Equipment (laptop, monitor, external drives — depreciated over time)
- Client meeting expenses (coffee, meals — 50% deductible)
- Mileage for client visits ($0.70/mile for 2025 — keep a log with date, destination, purpose)
- Professional development (courses, books, tools for learning)
- Business cards, printed materials

**Mileage log format:**
```
Date | Start | End | Miles | Purpose
3/15 | Home (Manteca) | Rodriguez Plumbing (Stockton) | 28 | Client kickoff meeting
```
