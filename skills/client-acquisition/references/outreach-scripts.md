# Outreach Scripts Reference

Full scripts for all five outreach channels. Always run the pre-outreach audit before using any of these — your specific observation is what makes these work.

---

## General Rules (All Channels)

- Lead with the observation, not the pitch
- Short — 3–5 sentences for first contact
- One CTA — ask for a 15-minute call or reply
- No fake urgency
- Personalize every message — generic blasts get ignored
- Professional but human — you are a person, not a corporation

---

## 1. Cold Email

**Subject lines (test these — subject line is everything):**
- "Quick question about [Business Name]'s website"
- "Noticed something on [Business Name]'s site"
- "[Business Name] — website feedback"
- "Your website on mobile"
- "Did you know [Business Name] isn't showing up on Google?"

**Template A — They have a site with a problem:**
```
Subject: Quick question about [Business Name]'s website

Hi [Name],

I came across [Business Name] while looking at [service] businesses
in [city] — great reviews on Google.

I noticed your website [specific observation — e.g. "doesn't load
well on mobile" / "loads pretty slowly" / "doesn't show up when I
search [service] [city]"]. For a business with your reputation,
that's probably costing you customers who find you on their phone
and leave.

I'm [Your Name], a web designer based in [Your City, ST]. I build custom
websites for local businesses — no templates, no platform lock-in,
you own the code.

Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call this week? I'd be happy
to show you what I found and what a better site could look like.

[Your Name]
[your phone]
[your email]
[portfolio — to be added]
```

**Template B — They have no website:**
```
Subject: [Business Name] — no website?

Hi [Name],

I was searching for [service] in [city] and came across your Google
listing — solid reviews. But I noticed you don't have a website.

Most people searching for [service] on their phone expect to find
a site before calling. Without one, you're likely losing customers
to competitors who have one — even if your work is better.

I'm [Your Name], a web designer based in [Your City, ST]. I build custom
websites for local businesses — fast, mobile-friendly, built to
show up on Google. No Wix, no monthly platform fees — you own it.

Would a 15-minute call this week make sense? No pressure — just
a conversation.

[Your Name]
[your phone]
[your email]
```

**Template C — [a higher-cost metro] / more professional tone:**
```
Subject: Website feedback for [Business Name]

Hi [Name],

I came across [Business Name] while researching [industry]
businesses in [city]. Your work looks excellent — I can see
why you have strong reviews.

I wanted to flag that your website [specific observation]. In a
competitive market like [city], a site that doesn't perform well
on mobile or load quickly can quietly cost you inquiries.

I'm a product designer based in [Your City, ST], currently working
with [a higher-cost metro] businesses on custom websites — designed around your
specific customers, built to rank locally, delivered as code you
fully own.

If you'd like a quick, no-obligation look at what's holding your
current site back, just reply or call me at [your phone].

[Your Name]
[your email]
```

**Email best practices:**
- Send Tuesday–Thursday, 8–10am or 1–3pm (highest open rates)
- Follow up exactly once, 4–5 days later if no reply
- Keep the follow-up shorter than the original
- A professional email address [name]@[yourdomain].com is the goal — Gmail works for now

## 2. Cold Call / Phone

The goal of a cold call is not to sell the project — it is to earn a discovery call. Keep it short, warm, and specific.

**Before you call:**
- Know their business name, what they do, and the specific issue you noticed
- Have your calendar open to book a time immediately if they say yes
- Call during business hours — avoid obvious rush times (restaurant at noon, gym at 6pm)

**Opening script:**
```
"Hi, is this [Name]? / Can I speak with the owner?

Great — my name's the user, I'm a web designer based in [your city].
I'm calling because I came across [Business Name] online and noticed
[specific observation — e.g. 'your site doesn't come up when I
search [service] in [city]' / 'your website doesn't load well on
phones'].

I work with local businesses on their websites and thought it might
be worth a quick conversation — would you have 10–15 minutes
sometime this week for a call?"
```

**If they ask "what does it cost?":**
```
"It really depends on what you need — every project is different.
That's why I'd love a quick call first, just to understand your
business and what you're looking for. Then I can give you a
realistic sense of what makes sense. Does [day] work for you?"
```

**If they say "I'm busy right now":**
```
"No problem at all — when would be a better time to reach you?
I can call back whenever works for you."
```

**If they say "I already have a website":**
```
"Totally understand. Out of curiosity — are you happy with how
it's performing? A lot of businesses I talk to have a site but it's
not really bringing in new customers. If you're all set, no worries
at all — but if you've thought about it, I'd love to take a look
and share what I find."
```

**If they say "Not interested":**
```
"No problem — I appreciate you taking the call. If you ever want
a second opinion on your web presence, feel free to reach out.
Have a great day."
```

Hang up. Mark as a no. Move on. Never push.

## 3. Text / SMS

Use for warm leads — people you've briefly met, spoken to, or who've been referred. Cold SMS to strangers can feel invasive. Use with judgment.

**Template A — After a brief in-person interaction:**
```
Hey [Name], this is the user — we spoke briefly at [location/event].
I build websites for local businesses in the area. I took a quick
look at [Business Name]'s site and had a few thoughts.

Would a 10-minute call this week work?
```

**Template B — Warm referral:**
```
Hi [Name], I'm [Your Name] — [mutual contact] mentioned you might be
looking to update your website. I build custom sites for local
businesses — no templates, you own the code.

Would you be open to a quick chat this week?
```

**Template C — Follow-up after no email reply:**
```
Hi [Name], I sent you an email a few days ago about [Business
Name]'s website. Just wanted to make sure it didn't get buried.
Would a quick call make sense?
```

**SMS rules:**
- Never before 9am or after 7pm
- Identify yourself in the first line — they may not have your number
- One message per outreach attempt — no double-texting cold contacts
- Keep it under 160 characters if possible

## 4. Instagram / Social DM

Works well for visually-oriented businesses — photographers, restaurants, salons, landscapers, artists. They're already on the platform engaging with their brand.

**Before DMing:**
- Follow their account first, wait a day or two
- Like 2–3 recent posts genuinely — only if you see something worth commenting on
- This warms the interaction slightly

**Template A — Creative / visual business:**
```
Hey [Name] — love the work you're putting out. Genuinely great.

I'm [Your Name], a web designer. I noticed your site doesn't quite do
your work justice — [specific observation e.g. 'hard to navigate
on mobile' / 'portfolio doesn't load well'].

Would love to show you what a site that matches the quality of your
work could look like. Open to a quick chat?
```

**Template B — Service business:**
```
Hi [Name] — came across [Business Name] while looking at [service]
businesses in [city]. Your reviews are impressive.

Noticed your website [specific issue]. For a business with your
reputation, that's probably costing you inquiries.

I'm a web designer in [your city]. Would a short call to walk through
what I found make sense?
```

**Template C — No website:**
```
Hi [Name] — I follow [Business Name] here and your work is
genuinely great. Noticed you don't have a website though.

A lot of people find businesses on Google before Instagram. Without
a site, you're invisible to that audience.

I build custom sites for local businesses — no templates, no
platform fees, you own the code. Would love to chat if you're open.
```

**DM rules:**
- Keep it short — DMs are conversational, not email-length
- Don't pitch price or scope in the DM — get the call first
- Respond quickly if they reply
- If no reply after one message, leave it — don't follow up on DMs

## 5. In Person / Door to Door

Highest conversion channel for local small businesses. A face and a handshake builds more trust in 2 minutes than 20 emails. It takes courage and becomes natural fast.

**What to bring:**
- A simple business card (name, "Web Designer," email, phone, portfolio URL when ready)
- Examples on your phone — something to show
- A notepad to write down their info
- Confidence — you are offering something genuinely valuable

**How to approach:**
- Walk in during a slow time — not during a rush
- Ask to speak with the owner — not an employee who can't decide
- If the owner isn't there, get their name and ask when they're usually in

**Opening script:**
```
"Hi — I'm [Your Name], I'm a web designer based here in [city].
I was in the area and stopped in because I came across your
business online and noticed [specific observation].

I work with local businesses on their websites — building them
from scratch or improving what's already there. I wanted to
introduce myself and see if it's worth a conversation.

Do you have 5 minutes, or is there a better time I could come back?"
```

**If they engage:**
```
"I took a quick look at your current site. [Share 1–2 specific
observations.] What I do is build custom websites — not templates,
you own the code, no monthly fees to a platform.

The goal is a site that actually brings in new customers — shows
up on Google when people search [service] in [city], looks great
on mobile, and makes it easy for people to contact you.

Would it make sense to set up a short call this week so I can
learn more about what you're looking for?"
```

**Leave something behind:**
Even if they're not ready, leave a card. Local business owners talk to each other — a referral from one to another is worth 10 cold emails.

**In-person rules:**
- Warm, not salesy — you're having a conversation, not delivering a pitch
- Read the room — if they're clearly busy, offer to come back
- Never pushy — confident and relaxed converts better than urgency
- Dress professionally but approachably — clean and neat

## Follow-Up Sequences

**Email follow-up 1 (4–5 days after first email):**
```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Hi [Name],

Just following up on my note from earlier this week — wanted to
make sure it didn't get buried.

If now isn't the right time, no worries at all. But if you're open
to a quick look at your website, I'd love to share what I found.

[Your Name]
[your phone]
```

**Email follow-up 2 (7–10 days after follow-up 1 — only if they've shown interest):**
```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Hi [Name],

Last follow-up from me — I know inboxes get busy.

If improving your web presence becomes a priority, I'd love to help.
You can reach me at [your phone] or just reply here.

Wishing [Business Name] continued success.

[Your Name]
```

After two follow-ups with no response — move on. Archive and revisit in 3–6 months.

**Phone follow-up:**
- Wait 2–3 business days before calling again after a voicemail
- If no answer on the second call, switch to email or text instead
- Never leave more than 2 voicemails
