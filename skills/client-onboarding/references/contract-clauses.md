# Contract Clauses

Exact language for the critical clauses every freelance web design agreement needs. Copy these directly into your agreement — email confirmation or a simple one-page document both work. You do not need a lawyer for projects at this scale.

**Tools to send agreements:**
- **Bonsai** (~$17/month) — generates contracts, gets e-signatures, connects to invoicing
- **HelloSign / DocuSign** — free tiers for e-signatures if you write your own doc
- **Email** — a plain email reply from the client saying "I agree to the terms" is legally binding in California for contracts under $500. For larger projects, get a signature.

---

## 1. Scope of Work

Define exactly what is included — and explicitly what is not.

> "This agreement covers the design and development of the following pages for [Business Name]:
> [List every page]
>
> Included: Mobile-responsive design, basic on-page SEO setup, WCAG 2.2 AA accessibility compliance, [X] rounds of revisions, and a README file documenting the project.
>
> Not included: Copywriting, photography, logo design, ongoing maintenance, additional pages beyond those listed above, SEO campaigns, or any features not described in this agreement. Any work outside this scope requires a written change order."

---

## 2. Payment Terms

> "Total project investment: $[amount]
>
> Payment schedule:
> - 50% deposit ($[amount]) due before work begins. Work will not start until deposit is received and cleared.
> - 50% final payment ($[amount]) due upon project completion, before files are delivered or the site goes live.
>
> Invoices unpaid after 14 days of the due date are subject to a late fee of $25 or 5% of the invoice total, whichever is greater.
>
> Accepted payment methods: Zelle to (408) 759-0778, PayPal to dereck.villagrana58@gmail.com, or as otherwise agreed."

---

## 3. Revision Rounds

> "This project includes [2] rounds of revisions. A revision round is defined as one consolidated set of feedback submitted in a single document or message, covering changes to existing content and layout within the agreed scope.
>
> A revision round does NOT include: new pages not in the original scope, changes to the overall design direction after it has been approved, addition of new features or functionality, or rewriting of content provided by the client.
>
> Additional revision rounds beyond those included are billed at $75/hour. A change order will be sent for approval before additional work begins."

---

## 4. IP Ownership — Tied to Full Payment

> "All work product created by Dereck Villagrana — including designs, code, graphics, and written content — remains the intellectual property of the designer until full and final payment has been received.
>
> Upon receipt of final payment, all intellectual property rights, including ownership of all code and design files, transfer to [Client Name / Business Name]. The client may not publish, deploy, or use any deliverables in any form until final payment is confirmed."

**What this means in practice:** If they don't pay, the site doesn't go live. You can take it down. Never hand over files or push a site live before the final invoice clears.

---

## 5. Kill Fee / Cancellation

> "In the event the client cancels this project after work has begun, the following applies:
>
> - The deposit is non-refundable in all cases.
> - The client agrees to pay for all work completed to the date of cancellation, calculated as a percentage of total project completion multiplied by the total project fee.
> - In addition, a cancellation fee of 25% of the remaining unpaid project balance is due to compensate for reserved time and lost opportunity.
>
> Cancellation must be communicated in writing (email is acceptable)."

---

## 6. Change Order Process

> "Any work requested beyond the scope defined in this agreement is considered a change order. Change orders require written approval before work begins.
>
> When a change order is requested, Dereck will provide a brief description of the additional work, the additional cost, and the estimated time impact on the project timeline. Work on the change order begins only after written client approval.
>
> Change order fees are added to the final invoice."

**Script to use when a client asks for something out of scope:**
```
"Happy to add that — it's outside our original scope, so I'll send
a quick change order with the cost before I start. Should be
straightforward. I'll have it to you today."
```

Never say "I can't do that." Always say "I can — here's what it costs."

## 7. Client Responsibilities and Project Delays

> "The client agrees to provide all required materials — including content, images, brand assets, login credentials, and feedback — within the agreed timeline.
>
> If the client delays providing required materials or feedback by more than 14 consecutive days, the project timeline will be extended accordingly. If the delay exceeds 30 days, Dereck reserves the right to reschedule the project start date or charge a restart fee of $150 to re-onboard the project.
>
> Dereck will communicate project status and milestones via [agreed channel — email / text]. The client agrees to respond to requests for feedback or approvals within 7 business days."

---

## 8. Warranty and Post-Launch Support

> "Dereck warrants that the delivered website will function as described for 14 days following launch. Any bugs or errors caused by Dereck's work will be corrected at no additional charge within this period.
>
> After the 14-day warranty period, ongoing support and updates are available through a maintenance plan or billed at $75/hour. This agreement does not include ongoing maintenance, content updates, or technical support beyond the warranty period unless separately agreed."

---

## 9. Limitation of Liability

> "Dereck Villagrana's total liability to the client for any claim arising from this agreement — including errors, omissions, or delays — shall not exceed the total amount paid by the client under this agreement. Dereck is not liable for any indirect, incidental, or consequential damages, including lost revenue or business interruption, arising from the use or inability to use the delivered work."

**What this means in practice:** If a site goes down after launch and the client claims they lost $10,000 in business, this clause caps your exposure at what they actually paid you. Without it, there's no limit. You don't need a lawyer to include this — it's standard language for freelance service contracts.

## 10. Simple Agreement Email Template

For smaller projects where a full document feels like overkill, this email + reply works:

```
Subject: Agreement — [Business Name] Website Project

Hi [Name],

Following up on our conversation — here's a quick summary of what
we've agreed to:

SCOPE: [List pages and key features]
NOT INCLUDED: [List explicitly]
INVESTMENT: $[total] — $[deposit] deposit to start, $[final] on delivery
REVISIONS: [X] rounds included; additional rounds at $75/hour
TIMELINE: Approximately [X] weeks from deposit to launch
IP: All files transfer to you upon final payment
CANCELLATION: Deposit non-refundable; work completed to date billed if cancelled

Please reply with "I agree to these terms" to confirm, and I'll
send the deposit invoice right away.

Looking forward to working with you.

Dereck
(408) 759-0778
```

A reply of "I agree" or "sounds good" to this email is a legally binding agreement in California for service contracts of this type. Email agreements are enforceable regardless of the project amount — the key is that the terms are clearly stated and the client's reply constitutes acceptance. For larger projects ($1,500+), a formal signed document is still recommended for extra protection.
