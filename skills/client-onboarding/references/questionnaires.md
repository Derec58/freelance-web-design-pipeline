# Client Questionnaires

Two versions: the full 44-question questionnaire for most projects, and a 14-question fast-track for simple or time-pressed situations. Always encourage the full version — more information leads to better designs and fewer revision rounds.

**Message to send with the questionnaire:**
> "The more detail you can share here, the better the result — I use your answers to make design decisions that actually fit your business and your customers. Clients who fill this out fully tend to be happier with the first draft. That said, if you're short on time, there's a shorter version below — just know we may need a few extra check-ins to fill the gaps."

**Example answers** are shown in *italics* throughout. They're there so you know what a good answer looks like — share them with clients who say they're not sure how to answer, or use them yourself to recognize when an answer is too vague to work from.

## Full Questionnaire (44 Questions)

### Section 1 — About Your Business

1. What is your business name (exactly as it should appear on the site)?
   *Example: "Rodriguez Plumbing & Drain — but just 'Rodriguez Plumbing' is fine for the logo"*

2. What do you do? Describe your services or products in plain language, as if explaining to a stranger.
   *Example: "We fix leaky pipes, unclog drains, install water heaters, and handle full bathroom replumbs for homes and small businesses in [a nearby city] and the surrounding area."*

3. What makes you different from competitors? What do you offer that others don't?
   *Example: "We answer the phone ourselves — no call center. We show up on time and send a text when we're 20 minutes out. Most jobs same day or next day."*

4. How long have you been in business?
   *Example: "11 years. Licensed since 2013."*

5. Where are you based, and what area do you serve?
   *Example: "Based in [your city], CA. We serve [your city] and the surrounding towns."*

6. What are your business hours?
   *Example: "Monday–Friday 7am–6pm, Saturday 8am–2pm. Emergency calls 24/7."*

7. What are your contact details for the site? (phone, email, address if applicable)
   *Example: "(209) 555-0182 | hello@rodriguezplumbing.com | No street address — mobile service."*

8. Do you have social media profiles? (List URLs for any you want linked from the site)
   *Example: "Facebook: facebook.com/rodriguezplumbing — we post there maybe once a month. Instagram we started but never really used."*

---

### Section 2 — Your Customers

9. Who is your ideal customer? Describe them — their age, situation, what they need, why they come to you.
   *Example: "Homeowners, usually 35–65, dealing with something that's already broken or leaking. They're stressed, want it fixed today, and want to know the price upfront before anyone starts. They chose us because a neighbor or family member recommended us."*

10. How do most new customers find you right now?
    *Example: "Mostly word of mouth from past customers. Some from Google — people searching 'plumber near me' or 'emergency plumber [a nearby city].' A few from our Google Business Profile. Almost none from social media."*
    *(Why this matters: determines local SEO priorities, where to invest on the site, and what trust signals matter most.)*

11. What is the #1 question or concern a new customer usually has before working with you?
    *Example: "How much is this going to cost? People are always worried we'll charge an arm and a leg without telling them upfront. We give a quote before we start anything."*

12. What do happy customers say about you? (Feel free to paste a review or two)
    *Example: "Our Google reviews say things like 'showed up on time,' 'fair price,' 'explained everything,' 'will definitely use again.' One review said: 'I called four plumbers. Rodriguez was the only one who actually showed up when they said they would.'"*

13. Have you ever gotten feedback — from customers, friends, or online reviews — about why people chose you over someone else?
    *Example: "A lot of them say it was the Google reviews. One customer told me she called us because we had more reviews than anyone else and they all mentioned we were honest about pricing."*
    *(Why this matters: this is Jobs-to-Be-Done gold — it tells us exactly what message to lead with on the site.)*

14. Is there a customer type you don't want more of, or a service you want to move away from?
    *Example: "We're trying to do less small apartment maintenance work — the management companies are slow to pay and always negotiate the price. We'd rather focus on homeowners and commercial accounts."*

### Section 3 — Goals for the Website

15. What is the single most important thing you want visitors to do on the site?
    (Examples: call you, fill out a contact form, book an appointment, buy a product, visit your location)
    *Example: "Call us. Ideally the phone number is the first thing they see, especially on mobile."*

16. Are there secondary goals? (Examples: learn about services, see your portfolio, read reviews)
    *Example: "Show that we're licensed and insured. Show our Google reviews. Let people see what services we offer so they know we do more than just drain cleaning."*

17. What does success look like 6 months after launch? How would you know the site is working?
    *Example: "More calls from people who found us on Google instead of just word of mouth. Right now about 30% of calls come from Google — I'd want that to be 50%."*

18. Do you have an existing website? If yes, what do you like about it and what do you want to change?
    *Example: "Yeah, it's a Wix site my nephew made in 2018. It looks okay on desktop but terrible on phones. The contact form never worked right. It doesn't really say why you should call us instead of anyone else."*

19. Who are your main local competitors? List 2–3 by name if you can.
    *Example: "Valley Plumbing, Ace Rooter, and there's a newer guy — [your region] Drain Pros — who's been showing up on Google a lot lately."*
    *(Why this matters: feeds directly into competitive analysis — we'll look at their sites to find gaps the user can own.)*

20. What do you dislike about competitor websites you've seen?
    *Example: "They all look the same — stock photos of plumbers you've never met, generic 'we're the best' copy that means nothing. None of them make it easy to just call."*

### Section 4 — Design and Brand

21. Share 2–3 websites you like (any industry) and for each, say specifically what you like about it. (Layout? Colors? How it feels? The photography?)
    *Example: "I like how chipotle.com is simple and direct — big photos, easy to find what you need, nothing confusing. I also liked a plumbing company I saw once in Texas — they had real photos of the owner and the truck, felt trustworthy."*

22. Share 1–2 websites you dislike and say what bothers you about them.
    *Example: "Our current site — hard to navigate on phone, the colors are dull, and there's too much text. Also dislike sites that have a pop-up the second you land on them."*

23. If you had to describe the feeling you want customers to have when they land on your site — in one sentence — what would it be?
    *Example: "I want them to feel like they found someone reliable who will actually show up and fix the problem without ripping them off."*
    *(Why this matters: this single sentence is often more useful than 10 brand adjectives — it captures emotional design direction directly.)*

24. How would you describe your brand personality in 3–5 words? (Examples: professional, friendly, bold, calm, premium, approachable, no-nonsense)
    *Example: "Reliable. Honest. Local. No-nonsense."*

25. Do you have an existing logo? (Yes / No / In progress)
    *Example: "Yes — it's on our truck and business cards. I have a JPG but not sure if I have the original file."*
    *(Note: always ask for SVG or high-resolution PNG. A JPG screenshot is not usable for a website.)*

26. Do you have brand colors or fonts you use already? (If yes, share them — hex codes, font names, or brand guidelines if available)
    *Example: "Navy blue and orange — those are the colors on our truck and uniforms. No idea what the exact codes are."*

27. Do you have photos I can use? (Product photos, team photos, photos of your work, your location)
    If no — are you open to having photos taken, or would you like me to recommend stock photo sources?
    *Example: "I have some photos on my phone of jobs we've done — before and after drain situations, a couple of the truck. Nothing professional. Would love to get real photos taken if that's possible."*

28. Is there a color or style direction you absolutely do not want? ("Nothing too corporate" / "No bright colors" / etc.)
    *Example: "Nothing that looks like a big national chain — we're a local family business and I want it to feel that way."*

### Section 5 — Pages and Content

29. What pages do you think the site needs? (Examples: Home, About, Services, Gallery, Pricing, Blog, Contact)
    *Example: "Home, Services (maybe individual pages for drain cleaning, water heaters, emergency plumbing), About Us, Reviews, Contact. Maybe a FAQ."*

30. For each service or product you offer, list them with a 1–2 sentence description of each.
    *Example: "Drain Cleaning — we unclog any drain in the house, from kitchen sinks to main sewer lines. Water Heater Installation & Repair — we install traditional and tankless water heaters and repair any brand. Emergency Plumbing — burst pipes, major leaks, sewage backups — we respond same day, including weekends."*

31. Is there anything about your business that's hard to explain or that customers often misunderstand?
    *Example: "People assume emergency calls cost triple. They don't — we charge a service fee but the labor rate is the same. Also people don't realize we do commercial work, not just residential."*
    *(Why this matters: these misunderstandings become FAQ content and objection-handling copy on the site.)*

32. Do you have existing written content (about text, service descriptions, etc.) or will this need to be written from scratch?
    *Example: "Nothing formal. I could write rough drafts but I'm not a writer — I'd want you to clean them up."*

33. Do you need a blog or news section?
    *Example: "Not really — I don't have time to write posts. Maybe something where I can share tips occasionally but it's not a priority."*

34. Do you need customers to be able to book appointments or schedule online? If yes, do you use any booking tools (Calendly, Acuity, etc.)?
    *Example: "A simple contact form is fine. We prefer to talk on the phone first to understand the job before booking."*

35. Do you need any special features? (Examples: online store, menu, portfolio gallery, contact form, map, price calculator, live chat)
    *Example: "Contact form, definitely. Maybe a gallery of before/after photos. A service area map would be great. Nothing too complicated."*

### Section 6 — Technical

36. Do you already have a domain name (like yourbusiness.com)? If yes, where is it registered (GoDaddy, Namecheap, etc.)?
    *Example: "Yes — rodriguezplumbing.com. Pretty sure it's on GoDaddy, my nephew set it up. I have the login somewhere."*

37. Do you currently have web hosting? If yes, who with?
    *Example: "It's a Wix site so I think hosting is through Wix — paying about $20/month for that."*

38. Will you need to update the website content yourself after it's built, or are you comfortable reaching out to me for changes?
    *Example: "I'd like to be able to update my own hours or add a new service without calling someone. But I don't want it to be complicated."*

39. Do you have a Google Business Profile set up? (The listing that shows in Google Maps)
    *Example: "Yes — we have 47 reviews on there, 4.9 stars. It gets us a lot of calls already."*

40. Do you currently get any website traffic, or run any Google/Facebook ads?
    *Example: "A little traffic from Google — I can see it in Google Analytics but I don't really understand it. We tried Google Ads once for about $200 and got maybe 2 calls. Stopped after a month."*
    *(Why this matters: avoids breaking existing SEO during a rebuild; reveals if analytics tracking needs to be preserved or migrated; surfaces whether paid ads are part of the picture.)*

41. Do you use any tools I should know about or integrate with? (CRM, email marketing, booking software, analytics, etc.)
    *Example: "We use QuickBooks for invoicing. That's about it. No CRM or email list."*

---

### Section 7 — Project Logistics

42. What is your target launch date, or is there a specific event or deadline driving the timeline?
    *Example: "No hard deadline, but ideally before summer — that's our busiest season and I'd like the new site up before then. So maybe 8 weeks?"*

43. Have you worked with a web designer before? If yes, how did it go?
    *Example: "My nephew built the current site — it was free but I had no control over it and he moved away. Before that nothing professional."*

44. Is there anything else I should know about your business, your customers, or your goals that I haven't asked about?
    *Example: "We're a family business — my wife handles the scheduling and my son just got his license and joined us. That family angle is something I'd like people to feel when they visit the site."*

## Fast-Track Questionnaire (14 Questions)

Use for simple projects (landing pages, 3–4 page small business sites) or time-pressed clients. Note that additional check-ins may be needed to fill gaps.

**Example answers** are shown in *italics*. Use them to recognize vague answers that need a follow-up question.

1. **What does your business do?** Describe it in 1–2 sentences as if explaining to a stranger.
   *Example: "We install and repair fences — wood, vinyl, chain link — for homeowners and commercial properties in Tracy and [your city]."*

2. **Who is your typical customer, and how do they usually find you?**
   *Example: "Homeowners, usually 40–60, who need a fence replaced or a new one installed. Most find us through Google or neighbor referrals."*

3. **What is the single most important thing you want visitors to do on your site?**
   (Call / fill out a form / book online / buy something / learn about you)
   *Example: "Call us or fill out the quote request form. We want to talk to them before they call anyone else."*

4. **What pages do you need?**
   *Example: "Home, Services (wood, vinyl, chain link), Gallery, About, Contact. Maybe a FAQ."*

5. **Who are your main local competitors?** (1–3 names)
   *Example: "Tracy Fence Company and a couple of guys who don't really have a proper site — just a Facebook page."*

6. **What feeling do you want customers to have when they land on your site?**
   *Example: "That we're professional and reliable, not just some guy with a truck."*

7. **Do you have a logo, colors, or fonts already?** (Yes / No / In progress)
   *Example: "We have a logo on our truck — I have a PNG of it but not the original file."*

8. **Do you have photos I can use?** (Real photos of your work, team, or location)
   If no — are you okay with stock photos for now?
   *Example: "Lots of photos on my phone of completed fences. Nothing professional but they look decent."*

9. **Share 1–2 websites you like and say what specifically you like about them.**
   *Example: "I like how simple and clean tractorsupply.com is — you can find anything fast. Also a fencing company in Sacramento had great before/after photos which I liked."*

10. **Describe your brand in 3–5 words.**
    *Example: "Reliable, local, fair price, no hassle."*

11. **Do you have a domain name?** (Yes / No — and if yes, where it's registered)
    *Example: "tracyfencing.com — on GoDaddy, I have the login."*

12. **Do you currently have a website or get any traffic from Google?**
    *Example: "Just a Facebook page. No website yet. We show up on Google Maps though — 23 reviews."*

13. **Will you need to update content yourself after the site is built?**
    (Yes → we'll need a CMS / No → I'll handle changes for you)
    *Example: "Ideally yes — just to update our gallery with new projects. Nothing complicated."*

14. **What is your target launch date and approximate budget?**
    *Example: "Before the end of the month ideally — spring is busy for us. Budget is around $800–$1,200."*
